What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services such as storage, software, databases, and servers over the internet. Becaue of different computing services, users can access different resources such as storing files or running programs from a remote data centers via internet connection.

Many people use cloud computing on daily basis without even realizing it. Services provided by different companies such as Google Drive from google, Microsoft OneDrive from Microsoft, Apple iCloud from Applie, and Dropbox allow users to save and access files online from different or multiple devices. Video streaming services and online collaboration tools are aslo services that rely on cloud technology to deliver content quickly and efficiently.

Differnet businesses benefit from cloud computing because it helps reduce hardware costs, improves collaboration, and allows users to work from almost anywhere. Cloud technology became one of the most important foundations of modern computing and because of this role and responsibility it will continue to grow.

Why Cloud Computing Matters

Cloud computing is important because it helps people store their files and use different online services. Instead of saving everything on one computer, users can access their files and applications through the internet. This makes it easier to study, work, and share information using different devices.
